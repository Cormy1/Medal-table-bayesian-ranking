Research

Ranking methods compared

Five ranking systems are computed and visualised in the dashboard. Each captures a different notion of Olympic "success".

Posterior Mean Rank

Expected value of the posterior rank distribution from the Bayesian hierarchical model. Accounts for uncertainty via partial pooling across nations.

Posterior Median Rank

Median of the posterior rank distribution — more robust than the mean to tail behaviour in the GEV prior. Useful for nations at the extremes.

GEV · Bayesian

U-Index (DEA)

A data envelopment analysis composite rank assessing medal efficiency relative to peer groups. Robust to outliers and covariate imbalance.

Non-parametric · DEA

Per-capita Rank

Normalises total medals by population (per million), giving small nations — Jamaica, New Zealand, Norway — proper recognition.

Normalisation

Total Medal Rank

The baseline raw medal count, ordered descending — provided to illustrate where Bayesian adjustment shifts perceptions most.

Baseline

Covariates

Hierarchical priors incorporate population, GDP per capita, and life expectancy (World Bank / WDI). Partial pooling across Games editions.

WDI · World Bank
